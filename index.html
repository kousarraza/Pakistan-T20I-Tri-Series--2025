Abstract

Cricket analytics has started receiving significant interest with the advent of structured sports data; this is because publicly available and reusable datasets representing international-level cricket tournaments are limited in nature. In this article, a comprehensive match-level dataset for the Pakistan T20I Tri-Series 2025 representing all the matches played during the series is presented. This dataset consists of comprehensive information regarding the match details, performance of both the teams, summary of scores, results of the toss, and the outcome of the match in a clean and well-formatted CSV layout with the sole purpose of facilitating reproducible research work in the field of cricket analytics.

Introduction

The application of data-driven methods to sports analytics has grown rapidly in recent years, particularly in cricket, where match outcomes are influenced by complex interactions among teams, players, conditions, and strategies. Despite this growth, access to well-structured, openly available datasets for international cricket tournaments remains limited, often restricting reproducibility and comparative analysis.

Importance of the Project

To address this gap, we present a complete and structured dataset for the Pakistan T20I Tri-Series 2025. The dataset captures match-level information across the entire tournament and is designed to be immediately usable for exploratory analysis, statistical modeling, and machine learning tasks.

Methodology

Data was collected from official match scorecards and compiled into a structured CSV format. Exploratory Data Analysis was performed using Python (Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, Seaborn). Statistical analysis included descriptive statistics, correlation analysis, and trend analysis across tournament stages.

Applications

This dataset enables multiple research applications:

  • Predictive Modeling: Match outcome prediction using machine learning
  • Player Performance Analysis: Identify key performance indicators
  • Strategic Insights: Analyze toss decisions and batting/bowling strategies
  • Comparative Studies: Compare team performance across tournaments
  • Educational Use: Teaching data science with sports analytics
