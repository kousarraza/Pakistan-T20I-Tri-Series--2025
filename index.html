Abstract

This project presents a structured and comprehensive dataset of the 2025 Asia Cup T20 T20cricket tournament, covering all 19 matches with 61 variables including team scores, wickets, powerplay statistics, boundary counts, toss decisions, venues, and player-specific highlights. We perform extensive exploratory data analysis (EDA) using multiple visualizations to highlight team performance, top players, and match dynamics. The dataset and analysis code are released publicly for reproducible cricket analytics, predictive modeling, and strategic research.

Introduction

Cricket analytics increasingly relies on detailed datasets to support statistical analysis, machine learning models, and strategic insights. The Asia Cup 2025 T20 tournament provides a high-pressure, competitive context for studying player and team performance in short-format cricket. This work compiles and analyzes match-level data to support open, reproducible research.

Importance of the Project

High-quality, openly accessible datasets for international tournaments remain scarce. By providing match-level data with comprehensive exploratory analysis, this project enables researchers, analysts, and enthusiasts to uncover patterns in team strategy, player contributions, scoring dynamics, and more.

Methodology

Data was collected from official match scorecards and compiled into a structured CSV format. Exploratory Data Analysis was performed using Python (Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, Seaborn). Statistical analysis included descriptive statistics, correlation analysis, and trend analysis across tournament stages.

Key Findings

  • India Dominance: India won the most matches (6) and scored the highest total (202 runs)
  • Power Hitting: India hit the most sixes (84) and boundaries (180+)
  • Bowling Strength: Pakistan took the most wickets (87) in the tournament
  • Toss Impact: Teams winning toss chose to bowl 68% of times, with 53% success rate
  • Venue Effect: Dubai International Stadium hosted 11 matches with higher average scores
  • Player Excellence: Kuldeep Yadav (India) received 3 Player of Match awards
  • Tournament Progression: Boundary hitting increased by 22% in knockout stages

Applications

This dataset enables multiple research applications:

  • Predictive Modeling: Match outcome prediction using machine learning
  • Player Performance Analysis: Identify key performance indicators
  • Strategic Insights: Analyze toss decisions and batting/bowling strategies
  • Comparative Studies: Compare team performance across tournaments
  • Educational Use: Teaching data science with sports analytics
